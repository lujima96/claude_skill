# Heroic Stylized Style Rules

## Style Dials

- `proportion`: broad shoulders, thick neck, larger hands, heavy jaw, strong forearms, grounded feet.
- `plane_simplification`: simplify secondary planes but keep skull, ribcage, pelvis, joints, and face construction.
- `feature_emphasis`: emphasize brows, tusks, jaw, hands, shoulders, and weapon or gear silhouette.
- `material_treatment`: separate skin, leather, metal, cloth, bone, hair, and eyes clearly in Godot.
- `detail_frequency`: keep low during blockout and primary forms; increase only after structure is approved.

## Non-Negotiables

- Joints still need to bend.
- Eyes still need eyeballs and lids if blinking or close-ups matter.
- Mouth still needs volume and closure if expression matters.
- Costume cannot hide missing rig logic.
- Silhouette must read at gameplay distance.

## Review Questions

- Does the style make the role clearer?
- Are exaggerations consistent across the body?
- Is the character still readable without textures?
- Are material families readable under neutral lighting?
- Would lower detail or LODs preserve the same identity?
