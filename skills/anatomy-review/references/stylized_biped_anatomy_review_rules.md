# Stylized Biped Anatomy Review Rules

## Review Order

1. Silhouette and center of mass.
2. Skull, ribcage, pelvis, and limb primary masses.
3. Shoulder, neck, hip, knee, elbow, wrist, and ankle landmarks.
4. Head, face, eyes, mouth, jaw, tusks, and ears.
5. Hands and feet.
6. Costume or gear interference with deformation zones.

## Stylized Orc Risk Zones

- Thick neck.
- Heavy jaw and tusks.
- Large shoulders and shoulder armor.
- Large hands and wrists.
- Broad torso to pelvis relationship.
- Hip and knee motion under belts or cloth.
- Face and mouth if snarls or phonemes are required.

## Review Questions

- Is the exaggeration supported by underlying structure?
- Does the body still have a clear center of mass?
- Are bend zones visible and plausible?
- Is the face built for expression or only for a still pose?
- Are hands and feet proportionally stylized but functional?
