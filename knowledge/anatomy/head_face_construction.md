## Construction Order

1. Cranial mass.
2. Jaw wedge.
3. Brow and cheek planes.
4. Eye sockets and eyeballs.
5. Mouth cylinder or muzzle structure.
6. Nose bridge, ball, wings, and nostril plane.
7. Ear placement or species-specific ear variant.
8. Neck attachment, throat column, and trapezius relationship.

## Stylized Orc Notes

- Tusks must emerge from a believable mouth and jaw structure, not float on the lips.
- Heavy brow and jaw can be exaggerated, but eye sockets still need volume.
- The mouth must support closed, open, snarl, and phoneme-like shapes if facial animation is required.
- Ears can be lengthened or sharpened, but placement should remain anchored to head construction.

## Deformation Requirements

- Eyelids must wrap the eyeball.
- Mouth corners need enough structure to smile, snarl, and compress.
- Jaw rotation must not collapse the neck or tusk base.
- Lip and mouth loops are required if facial animation or expressive posing is in scope.

## Review Checklist

- Front, side, and 3/4 views preserve the same head construction.
- Eyes sit inside sockets rather than on a flat face.
- Mouth has volume, corners, and closure path.
- Nose and tusks support the style without breaking face mechanics.
- Neck and jaw are connected as one functional structure.

## Common Failures

- Eye cutouts instead of eyelids wrapping a globe.
- Lips drawn as lines on a flat surface.
- Tusks pasted onto the face.
- Jaw made large without a clear hinge or mouth bag.
- Ears drifting too high, too low, or too far back.
