## Purpose

Use this when reviewing stylized biped proportion plans before blockout or sculpt detail.

## Stable Rules

- Stylization changes proportion ratios, not the need for structure.
- Lock the ribcage, pelvis, skull, shoulder girdle, hip sockets, knees, ankles, elbows, wrists, and hands before detail.
- Read the character at gameplay distance before judging close-up appeal.
- Exaggerate from clear primary masses instead of adding surface noise.
- Preserve deformation zones even when forms are simplified.

## Heroic Stylized Defaults

- Upper body may be broader and heavier than realistic reference.
- Hands, forearms, jaw, neck, shoulders, and boots may be enlarged for readability.
- Waist and pelvis may be simplified, but pelvis position must still explain leg attachment.
- Head size can shift for style, but skull, jaw, eye, and mouth relationships still need construction.
- Foot size should support stance and weight; avoid tiny feet on heavy characters unless intentionally comedic.

## Review Checklist

- Does the silhouette read from front, side, back, and 3/4?
- Does the stance show stable center of mass?
- Are shoulder width, ribcage volume, pelvis width, and leg spacing coherent?
- Are hands large enough for the style but still structured?
- Are knees, elbows, wrists, ankles, neck, and hips placed where they can bend?
- Are exaggerations consistent with role and gameplay function?

## Common Failures

- Huge shoulders with no ribcage support.
- Thick neck attached like a cylinder without clavicle or trapezius logic.
- Tiny pelvis that cannot plausibly support the legs.
- Arms treated as tubes instead of deltoid, upper arm, forearm, wrist, and hand masses.
- Detail added before the body reads in thumbnail view.
